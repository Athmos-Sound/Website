Quality & Usability

Our goal is to provide you with a sound package that strikes a fine balance between quality and usability.

Once we lock onto a specific athmosphere, our first step is to get the feeling right. We then proceed by boiling the athmosphere down to a set of modular sound pieces, focusing on usability.

Trough testing we determine if the package is good enough to release
keeping an eye on consistancy.

Composition

Composing your soundscape can be difficult when working with generic audio libraries.

Athmos tries to leveliate this by making sure the sounds are coherent across the board.

Below you’l find some techniques that can be applied to raze your soundscape to the next level.

Techniques

  • Titles

    Just like any chapter of a book is introduced by a title, we should do the same for our environment, use Titles provided in the package to introduce the player, this will align expectations and provide a clear starting point or transition to another environment.

  • Events

    Use Hits, Braaams or Bass Drops for impactfull events, if the event is sudden and quick a Hit or Bass drop can work, if the event is long lasting a Braaam could be a good option.

  • Intensity

    When the event can be predicted we can increase the impact by building intensity first, using a Riser.

  • Rithm

    Another way to increase the intensity is to build rithm, using Hits, or Bass drops, this can transition the player’s mood towards action and flow.

  • Combos

    Combine multiple Athmospheric loops togheter at different intensities to fine tune the feeling and intensity of your environment .

  • Layering

    Athmos provides modules, meant to be layered, layering can creatly increase the feeling and deph of your soundscapes.

  • Animation

    When combining loops it is possible to animate their intensity making the loop ever chaning or making the loop fit the level of intensity the player is experiencing.

  • Localization

    Environment drones are loops meant to tie the soundscape with the actual physical environment, use 3d sound for these, playing ocean near the beach, leaves ratteling near trees, wind on mountain tops.

  • Experimentation

    Don’t just take the things as they are.

    Try out
    reversing
    pitching
    reverb

    These will greatly improve your posibilities and make sure your project stays unique.
